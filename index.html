Custom GIS Solutions Reach beyond the basic GIS.

b. A new method was created to explore the reason why there is such a huge geographical disparity of total food cost across a three county area in Mississippi. A duel objective optimization problem was developed and implemented using python scripting to estimate the lowest cost based on the price of walking, public, and driving transportation and the food cost itself. It was found that transportation costs do substantially add a total overall cost to obtaining a healthy diet. In addition, rural residents were found to be paying the most for transportation and food prices compared to urban residents. The final paper is to be publish in an academic journal soon.

Web-based Solutions. Gather, analyze, and disseminate information on-the-fly.

Multiple GIS solutions were integrated into LocalFoodSystems.org. First, a mapping application was created to find business ideas in a user's area while being able to visual potential supply chain connections that were generated by a system algorithm. This allowed users to not only add their own business ideas and get results, but it immediately provided them with potential business partners that might have been right down the street. The second mapping application displayed and allowed overlaying of a number of GIS models of an agricultural asset inventory. The user could zoom into their backyard to see if they have the suitable area to grow grapes, build a solar array, or start up a biodigestor.

Data Management Big data problems. Big data solutions.

Years of social media data were cleaned and combined to provide insight into topic discussion, geographical relation, and communication. A relational database was combined with georeferenced IP addresses and multiple sources of location data to maximize the dataset while maintaining accuracy. These data were then combined with an unsupervised text classification analysis that generated common topics being discussed across the entire social media site. The result were multiple arrays of vectors that held multiple relations between thousands of people.
